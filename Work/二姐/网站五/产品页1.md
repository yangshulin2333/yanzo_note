##### Tethered or socket?

## Whatever you prefer, we got you covered!

Usually, tethered cable is preferred at home, while socket is more widely used in shared charging stations. However, this depends on the customer's preference.

![](https://d1lnencgr7glws.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/20094744/1024-Pulsar-Max-tethered.jpg)

![](https://d1lnencgr7glws.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/20094813/1024-Pulsar-Pro-socket.jpg)

Tethered solution

#### For convenience

While normally used at home, tethered chargers can also fit other locations. Key advantages are:

- Ready to charge from the moment you park
- No need to get the charging cable from the trunk
- Avoids potentially forgetting the charging cable
- No need to buy additional cable

Socket solution

#### For flexibility

The preferred option for shared chargers, but also selected by many home users. Key advantages are:

- Compatible with both Type 1 and Type 2 EVs
- Provides a solution if cable over 7m is needed
- Charging cable can be locked temporarily to become tethered
- Cable can be removed when not in use for safety reasons

Dynamic Load Management

Dynamic Load Management

Maximize charging speed while staying within your home's power limit. When overall energy consumption is high, charging speed drops. When it's low, you'll charge at maximum speed. That's energy, optimized.

- Avoid fuse tripping
- No costly upgrades or surprise energy bills
- Add a second EV, or more, anytime

Solar Charging

Solar Charging

Harness the full power of your solar panels. Avoid high grid energy prices when you charge your EV with 100% solar energy or a mix of solar and grid energy. The choice is yours.

- Maximized ROI on solar pannel installation
