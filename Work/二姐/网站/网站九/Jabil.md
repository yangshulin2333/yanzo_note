- **Shift to Integration**: Discrete compute, networking, power, and cooling elements must now function as tightly integrated systems at rack scale.
    
- **Operational Challenges Addressed**: Managing constant design iteration, compressed deployment timelines, and pressures from fragmented supply chains, tariffs, and geopolitical uncertainty.
    
- **Jabil’s Role**: Jabil provides engineering expertise and global scale, from manufacturing wafer fab and automated test equipment to high-performance compute, networking, photonics hardware, and integrated power/liquid cooling.
    

### Jabil's System-Level Solutions

#### Rack Integration

- **Core Delivery**: Engineering and building entire rack-level systems, including compute, storage, switching, photonics, power, and cooling.
    
- **Value Proposition**: Accelerating platform deployment, reducing total cost, and future-proofing against evolving architectural demands through flexible engagement models.
    

#### Liquid Cooling

- **Mission**: Enabling higher performance and greater density while reducing energy use as processors push thermal boundaries.
    
- **System Components**: Integration of cold plates, coolant distribution units (CDUs), and manifold designs.
    
- **Mikros Technologies Acquisition**: Broadens solutions with precision-engineered microchannel cold plate thermal management optimized for high-wattage compute.
    

#### Power Management

- **Efficiency**: Modular, integrated solutions enable power from grid to chip, reducing voltage conversion losses.
    
- **Hardware Portfolio**: Engineering and manufacturing of power distribution units (PDUs), uninterruptible power supply (UPS) systems, and low- to medium-voltage switchgear.
    

### The Jabil Advantage Across AI Hardware

- **Engineering-Led Engagement**: Tailored designs for hyperscalers and fintech platforms to accelerate time-to-market.

## PART 10: COMPANY HISTORY & CULTURE

### Our History: A Timeline of Innovation

Jabil’s cultural identity is rooted in its evolution from a small family operation to a global manufacturing leader.

#### 1960s: The Kitchen Table Origins

- **1966**: James Golden and William Morean form "Jabil," a fusion of their first names. The company begins by building circuit boards for Control Data Corporation on a kitchen table in Michigan.
    
- **1967**: Jabil receives its first official production order, moving beyond simple service and repair.
    
- **1969**: Jabil Circuit Company is formally incorporated.
    

#### 1970s: Establishing Industrial Scale

- **1970**: Jabil secures its second major customer, General Automation.
    
- **1979**: General Motors awards Jabil a **$15 million** high-volume PCB manufacturing contract, enabling the expansion of automated assembly capabilities.
    

#### 1980s: Technical Milestones

- **1982**: Headquarters are relocated to St. Petersburg, Florida.
    
- **1985**: Jabil invests in surface-mount technology (SMT) for highly automated volume production.
    
- **1989**: Launch of "box-build" operations (motherboards in plastic), moving toward complete assembly solutions.
    

#### 1990s: Global Expansion

- **1993**: Opens first international site in Scotland.
    
- **1996**: Expansion into Asia with a facility in Penang, Malaysia.
    
- **1999**: Acquisition of GET Manufacturing, Inc., adding **1 million square feet** of capacity and **5,000 employees**.
    

#### 2000s - 2010s: Diversification & Leadership

- **2002**: Entry into the defense and aerospace markets.
    
- **2007**: Acquisition of Taiwan Green Point (plastics/metals) and attainment of ISO 9000 certification.
    
- **2013**: Acquisition of Nypro, Inc., expanding packaging capabilities.
    
- **2014**: Establishment of the cloud data center business.
    
- **2018**: Strategic collaboration with Johnson & Johnson Medical Devices (JJMD), making Jabil the largest healthcare manufacturing solutions provider in the world.
    

#### 2020s: The Modern Era

- **2023**: Focus shifts to AI-powered cloud data centers; divestiture of the mobility business to BYD Electronic.
    
- **2024**: Acquisition of **Mikros Technologies** for liquid cooling capabilities.
    
- **2025**: Acquisition of **Pharmaceutics International, Inc. (Pii)**.
    
- **2026**: 60th Anniversary marked by the acquisition of **Hanley Energy Group**.
